Common Questions

Tax FAQ

Still have questions? Contact a tax specialist.

Most states require you to file a state income tax return if you file a federal return and earned income in that state. Some states (Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Texas, Washington, Wyoming) have no state income tax. Your state return is separate from your federal return but uses similar information.

For tax year 2025, the standard deduction is: $15,000 for single filers and married filing separately; $30,000 for married filing jointly and qualifying surviving spouses; $22,500 for heads of household. Additional deductions apply for taxpayers age 65+ or legally blind.

If you miss the deadline without filing an extension, you may face a late-filing penalty of 5% of unpaid taxes per month (up to 25%), plus interest on any unpaid balance. Filing an extension (Form 4868) by April 15 gives you until October 15 to file, but does not extend the time to pay any taxes owed.

File Form 4868 (Application for Automatic Extension of Time) electronically through our online filing system or by mail, postmarked by April 15. An extension gives you until October 15 to file. Note: you must still estimate and pay any taxes owed by April 15 to avoid interest and penalties.

Self-employed individuals and business owners may deduct home office expenses if the space is used regularly and exclusively for business. W-2 employees generally cannot claim the home office deduction under current tax law. Use Form 8829 or the simplified method ($5 per square foot, up to 300 sq ft) to calculate your deduction.
